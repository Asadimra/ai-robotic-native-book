# Chapter 1.1: Foundations of Physical AI

## Lesson 1.1.2: History and Evolution of Humanoid Robotics

### Explanations

Humanoid robotics has a rich history, evolving from early automatons and theoretical concepts to sophisticated machines capable of complex movements and interactions. Early efforts focused on mimicking human form and basic actions, often with limited autonomy. Significant advancements in sensing, actuation, and computational power have propelled the field forward, enabling robots to perform tasks in unstructured environments and collaborate with humans. Key milestones include the development of bipedal locomotion, dexterous manipulation, and increasingly intelligent control systems.

### Examples

**Early Automatons (18th Century)**: Figures like Jacques de Vaucanson's "Digesting Duck" were mechanical marvels, demonstrating rudimentary life-like movements. While not AI-driven, they laid conceptual groundwork for embodied machines.

**Honda ASIMO (2000)**: One of the most famous humanoids, ASIMO demonstrated advanced bipedal walking, running, and interaction with humans, showcasing significant progress in control and balance.

**Boston Dynamics Atlas (Current)**: Atlas represents the cutting edge, capable of highly dynamic movements, complex terrain negotiation, and advanced manipulation, often utilizing deep learning for control and perception.
